Understanding Cash Value

Whole life insurance includes a cash value component that grows over time. Here's how it works:

Years 1-5

Initial growth phase. Cash value builds slowly as most premiums go toward insurance costs.

Years 6-15

Accelerated growth. More of your premium contributes to cash value accumulation.

Years 16+

Substantial value. Cash value can be borrowed against or used to pay premiums.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does cash value work?

+

Cash value is a savings component that grows tax-deferred over time. You can borrow against it, use it to pay premiums, or surrender the policy for its cash value.

What are dividends?

+

Dividends are a share of the insurance company's profits paid to policyholders. While not guaranteed, SecureLife has paid dividends consistently for over 50 years.

Can I stop paying premiums?

+

Once sufficient cash value accumulates, you may be able to use it to pay premiums through policy loans or withdrawals, potentially creating a "paid-up" policy.
